Floating Solar Farms Could Transform Singapore's Energy Landscape

Key Facts:

  • Singapore lacks major rivers and experiences low wind speeds, making solar energy the most viable renewable option.
  • Terrestrial space constraints in Singapore make floating solar farms a strategic choice.
  • Floating solar PV systems reduce freshwater evaporation through shade-casting.
  • These systems help manage ecosystems by decreasing sunlight penetration, reducing algae growth, and improving water quality.
  • Water availability near floating solar installations allows for cooling and cleaning of panels.
  • High upfront costs make solar farms expensive to set up.
  • Floating solar panels utilize water surfaces like lakes and reservoirs to produce clean electricity.
